Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and physicist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. His work laid the foundation for the widespread distribution of electricity and the development of wireless communication.

Tesla's inventions and theoretical work in the areas of electromagnetism and energy have made him one of the most famous and influential inventors of the 20th century.
